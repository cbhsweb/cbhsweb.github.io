Our Tasks
Task How It's Completed Who Does It
Photo Taking The yearbook staff has a few cameras that they can rent from the class room and take photos with. They can also use personal cameras. Photos need to be taken all over the school and should include as many people as possible. Photographer + all staff
Caption Writing Captions should be written to give in depth details of what it happening in the photo. Captions should always start with a verb and never simply state the obvious. If it is a sports photo, for example, you should include what was taking place, where it was, who is involved, and the statistics of what happened. All staff
Template Designing Template designing is the first thing done when creating the yearbook. The templates are made over the summer in preparation for the year. The templates must be consistant and match the theme, color scheme of the book, and be well organized. Editors
Interviews Interviews typically take place after a photo is taken. Questions are asked to learn more about the scenario of the photo. It is good to get detailed background information. It will come in handy when writing the photo caption. All staff

Throughout the year, the yearbook staff has multiple deadlines to meet. By these designated deadlines, staff members must complete their assigned pages and turn them in. The deadlines this year were challenging to meet due to the small number of people on staff. Some of the deadlines this year were only 2-3 weeks apart. For these deadlines, the yearbook staff had to take photos, write captions, interview students, and conduct polls. With the help of their teacher, Mr. Walters, they were able to meet these deadlines on time.

The hardest aspect to meeting the deadlines was finding what pages could be completed. Many deadlines were assigned for the first semester. A majority of pages in the yearbook are sports pages. More than half of sports take place in the spring season, so those pages cannot be completed in the first semester.
