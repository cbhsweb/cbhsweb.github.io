Welcome to Christiana's CBHS Photography Club Page




About the Club


Photography Club is a fun and exciting club where you will be exposed to and have the ability to learn different styles of photography, as well as some technical aspects of picture taking and editing. Whether you're a beginner or an expert, this will be a great experience! All grades are welcome!
We meet every other Tuesday in Mr. Carpenter's classroom, Building 5 Room 121.

Photographs taken throughout the year will be put into the CBHS Photography Club Photo Album. Your images can be printed and sold during fundraisers, or be included in your own personal designs, brochures, posters, and other works!

We hope you'll join us for interesting presentations, exciting guest speakers, and fun field trips!

Meetings


Each meeting the club will focus on a specific genre of photography.
Meetings will be conducted in the following fashion:
  1. Brief presentation about the topic
  2. Photographing the subject
  3. Sharing photographs among members

Schedule

Topic Date Description
Introduction September 4, 2018


The goal of photography club is promote students' creativity and personal expression and to give them the opportunity to develop their own skills and styles throughout the year. The club would learn about and photograph many exciting topics in consecutive lessons.
Exposure and Composition September 18, 2018


The members will about the exposure triangle, which includes:

  • aperture,
  • shutter speed,
  • and ISO.

They will be exposed to the rules photographers use to compose their images, including:

  • balance,
  • symmetry,
  • patterns,
  • angle,
  • perspective,
  • background,
  • leading lines,
  • depth and depth of field,
  • colors,
  • and rule of thirds.
Portrait Photography October 2, 2018


The members will learn about the base exposure settings that are used to produce a well-exposed portrait, as well as different lenses for cameras and their effect on an image, rules of composition that should be emphasized, focus and lighting, useful tips, and viewed a short video that discussed posing. They will collaborate with one another to compose interesting portraits.
Staging Photography October 16, 2018


The members will how to stage and photograph various items in two ways. The first method is known as product photography, in which the background is completely white in order to focus mainly on the subject. The other involves more creativity, as the photographer can choose an interesting background and include additional subjects in their image. We will experiment with various lights, objects, and angles, showing a more innovative style of photography.

Food Photography November 13, 2018


The members will use their knowledge of staging techniques as well as useful strategies to photograph food. Before Thanksgiving, we will practice photographing fall foods, including pumpkin pie, cranberries, and others, as well as fall decor for plating and styling. Members will arrange their plates both individually and in groups, cooperating and developing new ideas and techniques.
Sport Photography November 27, 2018


The members will learn about specific tools, such as a tripod, prime lens, and zoom lens, that are very important for clear images, as well as the equipment used by professional sports photographers. They will practice using exposure settings to successfully shoot people in action, as well as certain adjustments that would make their manual photography easier and of better quality. We will photograph different school sports in different settings.
Landscape Photography January 8, 2019


The members will photograph landscapes around the school. Before shooting, they will be exposed to important equipment such as wide-angle lenses and telephoto lenses, as well as important composition rules and examples, including the crucial rule of thirds, and using foreground, middleground, and background to create depth in an image. Afterwards, we will walk around the campus to find different landscapes.
Nature Photography January 22, 2019


The members will use various techniques to photograph nature, capturing both large landscapes and small subjects, as well as moving and inanimate objects. We will visit a garden on campus and then proceed to travel around the school grounds and near the skate park. These settings will provide multiple opportunities to capture interesting images of insects, plants, the sky, and landscapes.
