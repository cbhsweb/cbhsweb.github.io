History of the Club

The club was formed on March 10, 1905 at The Rising Sun Pub, Fulham Road.






In September of 1905, their new arena debuted with a 4-0 friendly win against Liverpool. For the first few seasons the player wore Eton Blue, the horse racing colors of club President Lord Cadogan. This was a much lighter color than the color the shirts are now. Chelsea had never moved away from the famous stadium on Fulham Road. In just a few short years the personality of Chelsea FC was established. They had become wealthy, ambitious, fashionable, and had an immense drawing power

The mangers were:
  • John Tait Robertson (1905-06)
  • William Lewis (1906-07)
  • David Calderhead (1907-33)

Some of the key players at that time were:
  • William Foulke (1905-06) - Goalkeeper
  • George Hilsdon (1906-12) - Inside-Foward
  • Jimmy Windridge (1905-11) - Inside-Left

The Chelsea Foundation

This foundation was formed in 2010. It brings together the Football in the Community and the Education department along witht he club's other charitable and community activities, including environment and anti-discrimination projects. It is one of the world's leading football social responsibility programmes. The Chelsea Foundation works on a broad range of initiatives focusing on employment, education, social deprivation, crime reduction, youth offending and much more.
